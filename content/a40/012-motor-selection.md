Going with an SR20DET. The only other motor I seriously considered was the KA24DE (also Nissan, same era). Honda K-series was on the list briefly but getting it into a RWD application is a pain, needs adapter plates and custom mounts, and it's overkill for this car.

The SR20DET wins because:
- Factory turbo. Stock turbo form makes 200-220hp, bottom end handles 350-400hp with supporting mods. Start conservative, turn it up later if the car can handle it.
- Comes with a 5-speed manual. Transmission and bellhousing are one package, no adapter hunting.
- Massive aftermarket. ECU tuning, turbo kits, intercooler kits, standalone engine management, all available and well documented. This motor has been in Silvias and 240SXs for decades.
- Compact. Fits within the A40's engine bay with room for exhaust routing.

The plan is to run it on a standalone ECU from the start. Clean wiring, no splicing into a Nissan body harness, tunable from day one.

Cooling will be a custom aluminum radiator in the stock location. The A40's grille opening is small so airflow might need a slim electric fan behind the rad.
