The Miata steering rack is too wide for the A40's frame. It'll need to be narrowed to match the reduced track width.

> The geometry design reference post covers rack narrowing requirements, Ackermann error analysis, and bump steer considerations for the narrowed track.

This post will cover the narrowing process once the front suspension geometry is locked in. Tie rod geometry has to be right or you get bump steer.

## Approach

The common approach for shortening a rack is to cut both the housing and the rack gear, then weld the housing back together shorter. There's a good [LocostUSA thread](https://www.locostusa.com/forums/viewtopic.php?t=2773) covering that process and the pitfalls. I'm fine welding the housing, but I don't like the idea of welding the rack gear itself. It's a precision ground surface and a weld there is a weak point under load. Instead, the plan is to remachine the rack gear to keep it solid billet with no weld seams.
