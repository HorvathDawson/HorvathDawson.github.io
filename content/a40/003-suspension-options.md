Originally I was going to do a Mustang II front end swap. There's a guy on one of the Austin forums — [Vern Scholz's build on Driving.ca](https://driving.ca/column/its-mine/its-mine-vern-scholzs-1950-austin-a40) — who did exactly this and posted a pretty detailed writeup. My plan wasn't to splice the OEM MII crossmember into the original frame though, it was to fabricate a custom front clip and then splice that into the A40 frame to mount the MII suspension. The second A40 I bought actually already had a Mustang II swap done on its frame, but that one used the OEM MII crossmember spliced in, so it was a head start but not the approach I'd originally planned.

But after measuring that spliced frame and looking at it more carefully I wasn't happy with it. A spliced frame with grafted-in crossmembers is a compromise. The welds are in structural locations, the geometry is dictated by where you can fit the MII parts, and you're working around the limitations of the original frame rails. I decided I'd rather build a completely new frame from scratch and do it right.

So then the question was: still MII, or a different suspension system?

Spent a lot of time on Locost 7 forums and old MGB build threads. A lot of those guys are using Miata (MX-5 NA/NB) suspension swaps. Double wishbone, compact, good geometry out of the box, and a massive aftermarket. Some of the builds I was referencing:

- [Slow Car Fast MGB GT build diary](https://www.slowcarfast.com/MG/diary.php?start=160) — 1972 MGB GT with full Miata suspension integration, custom subframe, nearly 400hp on stock weight. 648-entry diary covering the entire swap in detail
- [Miata subframe and front suspension swap on MGexp](https://www.mgexp.com/forum/mg-engine-swaps-forum.40/miata-subframe-and-front-suspension-swap.2882197/) — Robert Yale's thread about grafting a complete Miata NB subframe and front suspension into a '77 MGB roadster. Good discussion on the chassis surgery required, shock tower placement, and whether to build a custom subframe to mount the Miata suspension points vs cutting the unibody
- [Emulating Miata suspension in VSusp on LocostUSA](https://www.locostusa.com/forums/viewtopic.php?t=17490) — Jeremy15 modeling Miata pickup points in VSusp for a BEC Locost build. Detailed back-and-forth on roll center migration through body roll, FVSA length targets (100-150" per Herb Adams), and how upper arm angle affects camber curve. Directly relevant to translating Miata geometry onto a custom tube frame

My car is narrower than any of those builds so it's more complicated, but the core idea still works.

Going with Miata suspension also lines up with the rest of the build:
- SR20DET motor means the car is already JDM-flavored, Miata suspension fits that theme
- Miata hubs are 4x100 bolt pattern, which means good disc brake options at all four corners
- Huge cheap aftermarket for bushings, ball joints, tie rods, everything
- The hub and knuckle assembly is compact and well-designed

MII would have worked. But Miata gives me better brakes, a better hub system, more aftermarket support, and a suspension geometry I can actually tune properly for this car's width. That's the direction.
