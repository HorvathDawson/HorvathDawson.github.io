Built a frame jig to keep everything square and level during the frame welding. Used I-beam for the base, two parallel rails running the length of the frame, with three cross members tying them together.

![Frame jig layout](/assets/projects/a40-austin/blog/frame-jig-1.jpg)

![Frame jig detail](/assets/projects/a40-austin/blog/frame-jig-2.jpg)

Tacked the cross members first after a lot of leveling and checking with multiple bubble levels. Once everything was confirmed square and plumb it got fully welded out. The jig has to be dead flat, square, and level. If the jig is off, the frame is off, and everything that bolts to the frame is off. This is the most important part of the whole process.
