Before doing any narrowing work I needed to get the actual dimensions of the MX-5 NA/NB front suspension. Stock numbers, pickup points, control arm lengths, spindle geometry. This is the baseline that everything else gets measured against.

Collected dimensions from a combination of the Miata factory service manual, measurements off a donor subframe, and cross-referencing with what other people have published on forums.

Key numbers I needed:
- Upper and lower control arm lengths (pivot to ball joint)
- Upper and lower ball joint spacing on the knuckle
- Spindle height and kingpin inclination
- Stock track width (center of tire to center of tire)
- Spring/damper mounting locations relative to the lower arm
- Steering rack mounting width and tie rod lengths

The stock Miata front track is about 1405mm. The A40's front wheel wells can accept somewhere around 1150-1200mm depending on how aggressive you get with clearances. So roughly 200mm needs to come out of the track width. That's a lot.

All of this goes into Suspension Analyzer V2 as the starting point for the narrowing work.
